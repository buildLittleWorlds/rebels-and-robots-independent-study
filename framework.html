The Rebels and Robots Framework

Introduction

This section provides the theoretical underpinnings of the "Rebels and Robots" system, explaining its rationale and key components. The system is designed to make it possible to understand and analyze character interactions with modern social and systemic forces.

Social Friction vs. Fit

This tension addresses the degree to which a character moves smoothly within their social environment, ranging from seamless integration to constant conflict.

Surface vs. Depth Engagement

This tension focuses on how deeply a character engages with the systems that govern their world, ranging from following explicit rules to seeking deeper understanding.

Character Tendencies

Based on the two core tensions, here are the five character tendencies of the system.

  • The Robot Tendency
  • The Genius Tendency
  • The Monster Tendency
  • The Rebel Tendency
  • The Coordinator Tendency

Modern Context

The framework is particularly relevant to modern literature, focusing on technology, bureaucracy, and societal change. These ideas form the background context for analyzing the characters in our mini-canon.
